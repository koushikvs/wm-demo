Act 1 · The trigger

A storm in New York. A crisis in Orlando.

Two days ago, the SAP Joule weather agent on BTP detected a severe winter storm system tracking toward the Northeast. Within minutes, it cross-referenced DC-North's 847 open outbound deliveries against regional SLAs and recommended an emergency stock transfer. Trailers began loading that evening. Thirty-six hours later, the first wave arrives at DC-South — just as the storm makes landfall.

Act 2 · The cascade

Too much in. Nothing through. Orders waiting.

🔴 Putaway Utilization 92%
Full Pallet storage type ZSTP-01 approaching maximum capacity. Emergency inbound surge from DC-North: 34 trailers dispatched, 12 already docked. Approaching bin capacity limit — putaway tasks queuing at receiving dock.
🔴 Replenishment WTs: 0/hr
Case/Each storage type ZSTP-02 has available capacity but zero warehouse tasks generating. All 12 workers allocated to emergency putaway — none assigned to replenishment. Pick face fill rate: 34%. Pick throughput: 0 lines/hr. Pickers idle — no stock in pick face.
🔴 Outbound Aging: 23 Orders
23 outbound deliveries past pick SLA (SLA window: 4 hours). Customer promise dates at risk. Pick bins empty. On-time delivery: 61%. Critical customer accounts affected.

The gridlock: Full Pallet Area [92% 🔴] blocks all putaway → replenishment stalls → pick face starves → aging orders accumulate.

Act 3 · The agent

Root cause identified. Five recommendations, ranked by impact.

🟢 HIGH IMPACT
A. Create Temporary Overflow Bins
Designate 16 trailer yard staging positions (Y-01 through Y-16) as temporary storage type ZSTP-TMP. Creates ~400 additional pallet slots. Relieves putaway pressure immediately. Estimated capacity relief: +17%. Enables continuous inbound docking.
🟢 HIGH IMPACT
B. Reprioritize Labor & Generate Replenishment WTs
Reallocate 4 of 12 putaway workers to replenishment. Generate immediate WTs for top 40 SKUs ranked by outbound delivery urgency. Restores baseline replenishment rate of 4 WTs/hr. Pick face recovery projected within 2–3 hours.
🟡 MEDIUM IMPACT
C. Increase Replenishment Quantity
Current EWM replenishment config: 1 HU per warehouse task. Forklift and aisle width support 2 HU per trip. Doubling the HU-per-WT parameter means each completed task moves 2× the material — 4 WTs/hr × 2 HU = 8 HU/hr, double the baseline without adding labor.
🟡 MEDIUM IMPACT
D. Authorize Overtime / Additional Shift
Based on 34 inbound trailers and 48-hour surge forecast, recommend 4-hour overtime extension for current shift plus on-call activation. Estimated additional throughput: +35%. Labor cost impact: ~$1.8K (8 associates × 4h OT at 1.5× rate).
🔴 FALLBACK ONLY
E. Direct Pick from Full Pallet Area
Allow pickers into ZSTP-01 for SLA-critical orders only. Suboptimal — pick path efficiency drops ~40% and elevated safety risk in narrow-aisle reach truck zones. Recommended only if Rec B fails to restore pick face within 3 hours.
